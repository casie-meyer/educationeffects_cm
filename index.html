The Effects of Education Data Classification

State's Population of Bachelor's Degree Holders

The first dataset that was classified was the percentage of each US state's population holding Bachelor's degrees or higher. The data is normally distributed, with a slight right skew from a small peak. Because of this, the dataset doesn't experience significant variance among states. This slight variance means that the best classification scheme for this dataset is the Jenks Natural Breaks scheme. This classification scheme works best to show the significant numbers of datapoints in the middle classes while also showing the variance along the number line. State's Population Living Under the Poverty Line

The second dataset that was classified was the percentage of each US state's population living under the poverty line. The data is somewhat normally distributed, with large peaks in the middle of the distribution, and very small peaks on both sides of the middling large peak. Because of this, the dataset experiences more variance between states than the previous one. This slight variance means that the best classification scheme for this dataset is the quantile scheme. This classification scheme works best to show the wider variations in the data classes while also showing the increased variance along the number line.
